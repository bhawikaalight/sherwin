Medical, Dental, Vision Coverage

Your eligible dependents include: Medical, Dental and Vision Coverage
Your legal spouse as defined by applicable state law. •
Your common law spouse as defined by applicable state law and subject to a verification process. •
Your domestic partner. The term “Domestic Partner” means: (1) person (of the same-sex or opposite-sex) with whom an eligible employee has a current valid domestic partnership registration, civil union certificate, or similar document from any state or local government agency, or (2) if no valid domestic partnership registration, civil union certificate, or similar document exists from any state, an eligible employee (of the same-sex or opposite-sex) is in a relationship with a person (of the same-sex or opposite sex) where they:
  1. have lived together for at least one year;
  2. are both age 18 years or older and mentally competent to enter into a legal contract;
  3. are both in an exclusive relationship;
  4. are both not married to anyone else;
  5. are both not related by blood closer than would bar marriage in the state;
  6. share the same regular and permanent residence with the current intent of doing so indefinitely;
  7. are financially interdependent on each other.
  8. were mentally competent to enter into the relationship when the domestic partnership began.
Please note: You will be asked to provide documentation which verifies your dependent’s eligibility for the benefit plans. Financial interdependency is demonstrated by the existence of the three (3) of the following (only 1 per category):
  1. Joint ownership of real estate property or joint tenancy on a residential lease
  2. Joint ownership of an automobile
  3. Joint bank account
  4. Joint liabilities (e.g., credit cards, utility bills or loan document)
  5. A will, retirement plan or life insurance designating the domestic partner as your primary beneficiary
  6. A durable power of attorney signed to the effect that you have granted powers to one another
 •
Your dependent children. Your dependent children are:
  1. Your children by birth, your stepchildren, and your domestic partner children;
  2. Children placed with you for adoption by age 18;
  3. Foster children or dependent children for whom you are the legal guardian**;
  4. Children you support under a Qualified Medical Child Support Order or administrative order.
Sherwin-Williams will determine whether or not an order meets the criteria of a Qualified Medical Child Support Order; or
  1. Your unmarried, dependent child of any age who is permanently and totally incapacitated, provided that the handicap began before the child reached age 19.
 Up to Age 26*

*Dependent children are covered until the end of the month the dependent reaches the age limit. Some HMO plans and have different age limits for coverage—please refer to   the Summary Plan Document of the plan for additional information.
**Foster or Legal guardianship terminates at age 18. Dependent children are covered until the end of the month the dependent reaches this age limit.
